Manhattan Hispanics are the most bivalent vaccinated for COVID, despite low percentage across NYC

Amidst the approaching winter, New York City struggles with low bivalent COVID-19 vaccine uptake among its Hispanic population.

By:Mariana Navarrete Villegas

Dec.14, 2023

As winter descends upon New York City, the urgency of COVID-19 vaccination is heightened, equity in availability and access is contended for the Hispanic community to have a full vaccination roll-out.

Hispanics in the Manhattan borough are the most vaccinated with a bivalent dose, which is essential to get immunity for the virus. But overall, it is alarming the disproportionately low number of Hispanics obtaining these crucial additional doses.

Community Health Worker from the non-profit organization Make The Road, Ingrid Barreda, has been doing vaccination campaigns across the city since August 2023.

“There is still a lot of misinformation or no information access for Hispanic community […]it is important for us to get to our people and tell them it’s needed a booster to prevent fatal effects from new variants”, said Barreda.

As the city navigates the seasonal surge, collaborative efforts must intensify to ensure equitable access, and safeguard the health of New York City's diverse population.


Data source: The New York Citywide Inmunization Registry
